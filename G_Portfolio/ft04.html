Mini Flamethrower MK4: Production
Fusion 360

#Prototype, #3Dprint, #CAD, #PersonalProject

As a reminder: This project is built around a lighter hack I found in a video 7 years ago by Jake Laser on his YouTube Channel JLaserVideo. The hack works by removing the fuel restrictor from a normal lighter so it dumps out all the fuel it can get through its nozzle in around 5-7 seconds.

Prototype Overview:

Whenever I told my friends about my flamethrower projects, the question I get asked a lot is "when can I get one?". The last few flamethrowers took months of planning and prototyping, so the goal with this one was to make it faster and easier to produce. There were also a few mechanisms I wanted to add to the flamethrower that I thought would help the functionality of the final product. To make iterating easier on myself I started prototyping one mechanism at a time. The one I had the clearest mental image of was the Cartridge Ejection mechanism.


Mechanism 01: Cartridge Ejection

The first mechanism I focused on was the cartridge ejection. I spent a lot of time before I started thinking out how I wanted this to sound and feel for the user interacting with it. I knew I wanted a pinch mechanism to release the lighter cartridge. I also wanted the reload to be quick just in case the user found themself in a dangerous situation and needed more fuel than one cartridge could hold. So I started with a lever and a spring.

The body was designed to be bent on the thin bridges called living hinges (close to the center of the model) so that it could be 3D printed with no supports. This saves on filament and print time.

After printing, the living hinges are bent 90° and bolted together as shown below.


Mechanism 02: Ignition Circuit Board

Another integral piece to this project’s functionality is the ignition; you can’t have fire without the spark. For this piece, I knew I wanted to activate the ignition with a slide switch. One of the problems of my last mini flamethrower was ease and control over the ignition. After 1 failed attempt at making this by trying to bolt the PCB on the side of the body, I decided to ditch the screws and lock it in utilizing the symmetry of the two halves of the body.

FAILED

Another notable difference from the last iteration is the addition of larger pads for the ejection pinch mechanism to minimize user error.


Mechanism 03: Gas Trigger

With the ignition set up, I got to focus on the trigger for the gas. In order to keep the size as small as possible I just made a trigger that extended the gas lever on the lighter towards my finger. I also decided to flip the side the pinch mechanism was on after a friend of mine said it might make the eject cartridge action a bit more fluid for the user. This made room for the Tesla coils above the ignition PCB board which will step the 3.7V from the battery to something that can make an arc of plasma to act as our ignition.
