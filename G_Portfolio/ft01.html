Mini Flamethrower MK1: Wrist-Mounted
Fusion 360

#Prototype, #3Dprint, #CAD, #PersonalProject

I started this project back in high school when I came across a video by Jake Laser on his YouTube Channel JLaserVideo on how to make a very small flamethrower with a very simple lighter hack. This version was essentially a proof-of-concept as my first attempt at making a mini flamethrower.


First Test Footage:


Prototype Overview:

Similar to Jake Laser's flamethrower, mine was also wrist-mounted because I wanted to be a superhero. My own version was made out of a TicTac container (this was before I had a 3D printer). Because of this this version of the mini flamethrower was double barreled (takes 2 lighter cartridges instead of 1) which was overkill so I scaled down in the next iteration.

Ignition:

In this version, the igniton button (located on the rear top of the body of the flamethrower) is turned on before use and stays on the whole time. This ended up being a waste of the battery life as the ignition is only needed to start the flame, after that the flame keeps itself burning. Additionally, the ignition only needed to be installed in front of one of the 2 lighter nozzles (gas output) because one ends up lighting the other during the fire throwing process.

Gas:

To engage the gas, I built a lever out of a bolt, 2 screws, and some metal I had lying around which I bent to be easily compressed by my middle and ring fingers. The hinge these pivoted on was made from a binder clip so I could lock the lever in the upright positon when I wasn't using it.



As you can probably tell this build was made from whatever I had on hand, but if the Iron Man movies taught me anything, it's one man's box of scraps is another man's working prototype.
