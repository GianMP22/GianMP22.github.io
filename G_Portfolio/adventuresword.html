Adventure Time Sword:
Fusion 360

#Prototype, #3Dprint, #CAD, #PersonalProject


Mark 1:

The first one I made of these was as a kid out of cardboard. I couldn’t find a picture but basically it was stacked cardboard pieces to give dimensionality and silver duct tape to make the blade look shiny. After the blade got a single bend in it, it became unusable.


Reference:

Mark 2:

Utilizing 3D printing I designed a handle that could house 2 flashlight LEDs running off of a large(ish) battery pack.

The blade was designed to be printed on a Creality Belt printer which prints at a 45° angle. This allows the printer to have a (theoretically) “infinite Z” axis which lets it print long models in one go without requiring the designer to cut their model into separate pieces.
