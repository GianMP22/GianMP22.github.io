Cura Infill Examples:
Fusion 360 & Cura

#3Dprint, #CAD, #GraphicDesign, #Work

While working in the 3D printing sections of my major's design lab (basically a mini makerspace), I found it hard to describe the different infills the Cura slicer offers without just showing the model sliced with each infill side by side. I wanted an easier way to show it so I made these infill examples by making a hexagon model - so that they fit together aesthetically - and setting the top layer size to 0 layers so it wouldn't print.


Plan:

This is a list of all the Cura infills I am using in this project.


I started by planning out how to display the hexagonal infill examples in illustator. I decided to go with the horizontal layout (the 2nd image).


Final:

After getting all the files 3D printed I layed them out on a piece of wood to be screwed down. Then, I laser cut the labels and inset them into their corresponding spots on the 3D model.


Animations:

The one thing these exmaples don't show is how each infill optimizes the movements of the extruder. For example, grid, lines, and zig zag all look the same after printing but the paths made by the extruder to make these patterns are all very different. I recorded these animations to show the difference between how these infills are processed.
