Gian Morris-Perez

Your friendly neighborhood tinkerer.

I use my knowledge of 3D printing and 3D modeling combined with my skills in physical fabrication to transform ideas into high-fidelity prototypes.
While my talent exists in the physical world, my education is from NYU’s Engineering school, specifically in Media and Design; meaning I have skills in digital entertainment/design, as well as CAD and other methods of manufacturing.

When it comes to the things I make, form follows function. What the invention does and how I want the user to interact with the invention constitutes its design. I create through trial and error, through endlessly tweaking my design until the invention becomes what it always wanted to be. Whenever I make something my designs don’t come from me exactly, the invention speaks through me, and I give it the form it wants within the constraints I deem necessary (plus maybe some stylish flair). For this reason, I don’t consider myself a dreamer, but I love to serve the dreamers, and through my hands their dreams can take physical form.
I love inventing, but dreaming is an integral part of inventing. Inventions are tools for us to interact with the world around us. The tools we have access to inevitably control how we live our lives.
It’s up to the dreamers to dream up a better life, to decide what tools this world needs, and it’s up to the people like me to make those tools a reality.
