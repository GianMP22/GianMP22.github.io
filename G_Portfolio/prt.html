Planet Real-Time YouTube Banner:
Photoshop

#GraphicDesign, #Work


Project Description:

While working as a Production Assistant for NYU's R-Lab under Todd Bryant, I was tasked to create a banner for his virtual 3D talk show Planet Real-Time. The only direction I was given was this a few clips from their episodes and the main theme behind the show: merging the real and digital worlds.
After going through the avaliable footage I picked out two frames in which the camera first showed a fully textured cloud and rainbow environment and then blinked to a wireframe version of the same environment. I split these two frames in half and made a pixeled gradient between them in photoshop. Then I overlayed the text "Planet Real-Time" in the center and used the same pixel gradient to split the lettering into a flat white side and a glowing side.
My idea was this image would represent "pulling back the curtian" on the digital world to reveal it's place in the real world. Embodying the message of the virtual production.
