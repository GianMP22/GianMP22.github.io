Brand Identity: CyberSync
Illustrator & InDesign

#GraphicDesign, #SchoolProject

I came up with this cyberpunk brand for a brand design class. The idea was: with the normalization of cybernetic implants looks are everything. But you don't want your newly installed matis blades to catch on your fashionable threads, so you need smart threads. New from the CyberSync collection.


Logo/Word Mark: Inital Iterations

I started by testing out some different typefaces to see what kind of styles I liked for the brand.

I liked all the outlined texts so I tested different iterations of 2 ways I could use the typefaces to design the CyberSync logo.

Design Iteration 01:

For the first logo design I used the typography of an C and an S to give the visual indication of a yin-yang symbolizing the synchronic behavior of the clothing with the user's cybernetics.

I tried the same design but with a hexagonal structure to make it more futuristic/tech-y. Needless to say this idea was scrapped pretty fast.

Along with the logo, I also played around with the different ways I could incorporate the logo into the word mark.

Shown below is the final design (with some color variations) I settled on for this iteration.

Design Iteration 02:

For the second logo I used the Audiowide font to create the logo by encasing 2 Cs around an S in the Audiowide font to make use of the negative space. The Cs interlock to imply a linked chain, again, symbolizing the synchronic behavior of the clothing with the user's cybernetics.

I liked the center logo so I decided to move forward with the new word mark with incorporated logo. As you can see, this is where I really started to play around with the use of negative space to suggest the "Sync" part of the name.

Shown below is the final design (with some color variations) I settled on for this iteration.

Bonus mockup:

One of the comments I got back on this design was the two different colors, while it made the "cyber" part of the name pop, it also made it less intuitive to read the negative space. People kept wanting to read the bottom colored lines as their own word, often forming "CyberPunc" - similar to the well known video game Cyberpunk which this project takes heavy inspriation from. The relation was cool but not at all what I was going for.

Inital analysis:

While the first iteration of the logo and workmark aesthetically look great, I feel like the second iteration works really well in mockups and fits better in a Cyberpunk distopia.


Logo/Word Mark: Finalizing

In order to get people to read the "sync" part more I dropped the dual color logo and settled for a single color for the wordmark. After that, I cleaned up the overlap so there was more distinction between the letters. Final iteration shown below.

I played around with some different ways to style to wordmark as well.
