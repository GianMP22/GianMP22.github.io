Mini Flamethrower MK2: Wrist-Mounted
Fusion 360

#Prototype, #3Dprint, #CAD, #PersonalProject

For my 2nd prototype of the Mini Flamethrower I focued on improving upon JLaserVideo's original 3D print. I made the ignition more intuitive and built the circuit into the main body of the flamethrower.


Prototype Overview:

As I said, this version of the mini flamethrower was just an edited version of Jake Laser's Flamethrower 3D model. I added cutouts to the main body to house the circuitboard and battery for the igniton as well as a few other modifications to the design, as seen below.

Original Design by JLaserVideo^

Re-Design by Gian MP^

Ignition:

In this version, the igniton button is turned on before use and stays on the whole time. This ended up being a waste of the battery life as the ignition only needs to start the flame, after that the flame keeps itself burning. Additionally, The ignition only needed to be installed in front of one of the 2 gas outputs because one ends up lighting the other. To engage the gas, I built a lever out of a bolt, 2 screws, and some metal I had lying around which I bent to be easily compressed by my middle and ring fingers.
As you can probably tell, this build was made from whatever I had on hand. But if the Iron Man movies taught me anything, it's one man's box of scraps is another man's working prototype.
